# Homeosetu Office Hours Notes

Date: 2026-04-20

## Business Profile

- **What:** Homeopathy education platform (courses + Kent Repertory clinical tool)
- **Stage:** Has paying customers, building toward MRR
- **Founders:** Dr. Alpesh Oza (16yr clinical + 10yr US software), Dr. Anshu Dubey (10yr practice, The Other Song Academy trained)
- **Distribution edge:** 10,000+ student surveys across 100+ colleges

## Key Decisions

### North Star Metric: Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)

### Business Model Reframe
- **Original assumption:** Courses are the core product
- **Revised model:** Kent Repertory (daily clinical tool) is the subscription anchor
- Courses = acquisition funnel (brings students in)
- Kent Repertory = retention engine (keeps practitioners paying monthly)
- Students graduate into practitioners who need the tool daily

### Competitive Position
- Homeopathy ed-tech is fragmented. No "Coursera for homeopathy" exists.
- Closest competitor: The Other Song Academy (premium, reputation-driven)
- Most alternatives: YouTube, Telegram groups, or generic LMS platforms
- Unique advantage: courses + clinical tool in one platform = stickiness

## Critical Gaps

### 1. No User Observation
Haven't watched real users navigate the platform without help.
**Action:** Watch 3-5 users (students and practitioners) try to find, buy, and use a course. No helping. Just watch and take notes. 45 minutes of observation > 100 hours of guessing.

### 2. Kent Repertory Positioning
Currently buried as a secondary nav item. If it's the subscription anchor, it needs flagship positioning on the homepage and dashboard.

### 3. Course-to-Tool Pipeline
No clear path from "I finished a course" to "I need Kent Repertory for my practice."
The transition from student to practitioner is where the subscription conversion happens.
